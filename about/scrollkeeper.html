The Scrollkeeper

Lover of books, music, and all things mystical

I am a man of diverse interests and expertise. My passions span the spectrum from natural history to the fine arts, and I have spent my life cultivating a deep appreciation for a wide variety of subjects. From a young age, I have had an unquenchable curiosity and a voracious appetite for knowledge, which I have honed through extensive reading, studying, and hands-on experience. My love for learning has led me down many paths and allowed me to experience things I thought once to be impossible.

I am first and foremost a devout Muslim. It is my opinion that the world in which we reside could not have come to be by chance; there is irrefutable evidence of intelligent design by the hands of an omnipotent, eternal entity beyond our comprehension: Allah, or as some may call Him, God. I believe that the Qur'an was revealed to mankind through the angel Jibreel, and that it carries the one Truth. The preservation of religion precedes the preservation of all other things in life.

Returning to my interests - they do not stop there. I am an accomplished programmer and artist with a keen eye for detail and a deep appreciation of the beauty of the world surrounding us. I am equally at home in the domain of philosophy and theology, where I have spent many hours contemplating the nature of the Divine and the purpose of life. I am an avid reader and writer, with a passion for poetry and literature, and I greatly respect the power of words to inspire people and instill goodness in their hearts.

In addition to my intellectual pursuits, I have a love for the physical; everyday I further sharpen my body to the same extent that I hone my mind. I enjoy spending time in nature, exploring the wilderness and discovering new things about the world around me. I greatly appreciate the natural world and wish to protect it for future generations.

Throughout my life, I have been deeply commited to the preservation and spread of knowledge. It is for this reason that I have created this Library as an archive of data that can be accessed and learned from by all, in the hopes that it will help restore the humanity of the Internet. I long for a return to the Golden Age, where the web served as a platform for sharing knowledge and building meaningful connections with its inhabitants. I see the current state of the Web as a far cry from that ideal, where corporations have taken over and inundated every page with advertisements designed to manipulate and exploit users.

As I reflect on my life's journey and the many paths that I have travelled, I am struck by the profound interconnectedness of things. The natural world, the divine, and humanity itself are all intertwined in ways that are beautifully complex. And despite the many challenges that we face as a global community, I remain hopeful and committed to the pursuit of knowledge and understanding.

In a world that often seems filled with division and strife, it is easy to become disillusioned and cynical. But I believe that there is still much to be gained from the pursuit of the Truth. After all, the end goal of reason and intellect is the perception of Truth, which is by extension the highest purpose that the human soul can hope to achieve (Al-Ghazali, The Alchemy of Happiness). By striving to understand and appreciate the creations of the Lord, we can connect more deeply with ourselves and one another.

Ultimately, I hope that my life's work will contribute in some small way to a brighter, more harmonious future for all, whether through my passion for natural history, my creative endeavours, or my efforts to archive and preserve the Truth.

As I continue on this journey, I am reminded of a quote by the philosopher Lao Tzu: "The journey of a thousand miles begins with one step." May we all take that first step, and may it lead us ever closer to attaining and perceiving the Truth.
